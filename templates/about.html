What is Reezy?

Reezy converts readings into summarized audio files of flexible length. For a student who has to read hundreds of pages every week, Reezy saves time and provides an easy method of refreshing what they learned. Reezy can also be used as an accessibility tool and is the only product that gives the visually impaired the ability to listen to summaries of any article or book.

How it works

After you upload the PDF file you want summarized and tell Reezy how long you want your summary to be, we convert it to a png file and then extract the text from the picture.

We then use an algorithm based on term frequency/inverse document frequency (tf-idf) to return your summary. The algorithm associates words with their grammatical counterparts (eg. goose and geese), calculates the frequency of each term in the text, assigns each word popularity points, figure out which periods pertain to sentence endings (eg Sgt. does not), splits the text into sentences, ranks sentences by the sum of their words' points, and returns X (based on the length requested). Reezy is best-suited to handle text-based files without tables or graphs.
